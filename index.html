About Me

I’m a Software Engineer (MSc 2020) from Portugal based in Berlin, currently at Klarna working on consumer banking products. I used to play professional handball in Portugal’s first division.

I enjoy playing with new technologies and expect (someday) to make useful software in the meantime. Automating sucker, also interested in Music Software, AI, and InfoSec enthusiast. I’ve previously worked as Junior Data Scientist at LTPLabs, Research Assistant at INESC-TEC, and as Teaching Assistant at FEUP for courses on Computer Graphics and Low-level Device Drivers.

Experience

Klarna

October 2020 - Present
Moved to Berlin as part of the Klarna’s 2020 Graduate Program. Working on the new consumer banking offering mostly as a Backend Engineer, focused on automating regulatory work.

LTPLabs

February 2020 - July 2020
Part of the development team of an internal product for Portugal’s largest retailer, Sonae MC. Focused on developing an ML methodology for sales forecasting, and to predict the impact of price markdowns on medium-long term sales.

INESC-TEC

January 2019 - August 2019
Part of the development team of MELOA as a member of the Computer Graphics and Information Systems (CGIS) department. Designed and developed a real time data streamer to authenticate, deliver, and load balance requests sent from IoT devices. Also designed a development kit for web apps that use MELOA’s data and additional logic by providing a GraphQL API with multiple data sources.

FEUP

September 2017 - December 2018
Assisted professors during undergraduate classes part of FEUP’s BSc. + MSc. in Computer Engineering program. Covered two courses: Computer Laboratory course (2017 and 2018) - dealt mostly with implementing low-level device drivers on a VM running Minix OS; Computer Graphics - a course consisting mostly of building guided projects with WebGL to learn the basics of computer graphics algorithms and structures.
